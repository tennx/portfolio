Sophomore Projects

Solar / Hydrogen Vehicle Project
  1. Project: Work in a team to develop a viable vehicle that can be powered by a hydrogen fuel cell and solar cell
  2. Course: POE (Principles of Engineering)
  3. Duration: 3 Weeks long
  4. Objective: To create the most efficient vehicle that could interchange hydrogen and solar cells to power the vehicle
  5. Type of Project: Group of four Project
    6.
  6. SUMMARY: I was assigned to work in a group of four with Will Lavanakul, Franklin Fitzpatrick, and Ian Yeung. We wre given three weeks to design a vehicle that could hold a power source to travel one meter in the fastest time. During the project, we first encountered a very fast prototype with our solar cell. Soon it stopped working and we had to come in during lunch to troubleshoot issues. Our primary idea was to use three wheels and place our motor in the middle and connect that to a chain and sprocket system to power our back wheels. We made many prorotypes since the many first didn't work. Although we didn't have the fastest vehicle in the class, we had learned a lot. We learned how to breadboard wires to make a working circuit, organize a document, and practice using Ohm's law. The project was very fun because there were little restrictions and I would've enjoyed more time to work on this project.

Project 3.2.4 Machine Control
  1. Project: Work in a team to develop a machine responsible for transporting cookies from point A to B to C.
  2. Course: POE (Principles of Engineering)
  3. Duration: 2 Weeks long
  4. Objective: To transport 3 cookies to one location (Secondary area) and detect when the 3 cookies are there, activating another part of the machine to deliver the cookies to the final area (Packaging area)
  5. Type of Project: Group of 3 Project
    6.
  6. SUMMARY: For this project, our group, Shelja, Ashley and myself were assigned to create a machine that would transport cookies to one location and then to the next. Initially, we were behind on this project since we were catching up from the previous one, so that required us to come in during lunch to work on it. To start the project, each member of our group created one design of a machine that we had in mind and compare it with each others' design. To narrow down our choice to one machine, we used a design matrix that would judge the ideas based on what our constraints were. For example, we were only allowed to use a certain number of sensors/motors so we had to take into account how much each machine required in order to work. We found that the best idea was Ashley's and began building that. Ashley had completed most of the program for the cookie transportation machine so Shelja and I had little to do but revise it. Instead, the three of us focused on building the machine and came across one problem. A type of transportation gave us issues when we tried testing our machine so we had to make a quick revision (the rack and pinion system in the photo to the right) to the machine so that it would work. Other than this edit, the machine functioned properly and was a success.
